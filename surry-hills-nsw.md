Surry Hills sits just south-east of Sydney's CBD, bordered by Darlinghurst to the north and Redfern to the south. Once a working-class neighbourhood of terrace houses and small factories, it has transformed over the past three decades into one of Sydney's most vibrant and sought-after inner-city suburbs.

The suburb is particularly renowned for its cafe and dining culture. Crown Street is one of Sydney's great food streets, lined with independent coffee roasters, wine bars, and restaurants spanning every cuisine. The Saturday morning crowd on Bourke Street — with its famous bakery and weekend markets — is a ritual for many residents.

Housing stock is dominated by Victorian and Edwardian terrace houses, many beautifully renovated but commanding significant price premiums. Apartments are more accessible, though still expensive by national standards. The rental market is competitive due to constant demand from young professionals working in the CBD.

Transport is a genuine strength: Central Station is a short walk, with trains connecting to all major lines. Bus routes on Elizabeth, Crown, and Foveaux streets are frequent. Walkability is exceptional — most errands, dining, and socialising can be done entirely on foot.

The trade-off is noise and density. Being close to the entertainment precinct means weekend nights can be lively, and some streets experience significant foot traffic. Families will find the suburb manageable but may prefer quieter surrounds as children grow older. Green space is limited compared to middle-ring suburbs, though Moore Park and Prince Alfred Park are both within a 10-minute walk.
